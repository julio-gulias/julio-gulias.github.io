Project Managing Your Job Search: Tools, Tips, and Inspiration.

A job search can feel uncertain and emotionally draining. Project management thinking can bring structure, focus, rhythm, and resilience to the process.

Job Search Project Management Career Strategy Tools

Originally published on LinkedIn on October 10, 2024.

As of today, October 10, 2024, it has been 9 months and 14 days since I embarked on an unexpected journey following the end of my last role in December 2023. This journey, marked by uncertainty and growth, has challenged and empowered me in ways I hadn't anticipated. Drawing on my Project Management background, I've navigated this path with purpose, using planning and adaptability to tackle the twists and turns of a prolonged job search. Through this series of articles, I hope to share insights, strategies, and encouragement that can support and inspire you on your journey.

Defining your plan

In ideal circumstances, a straightforward approach often leads to job search success:

  1. Define your target position.
  2. Update your job search tools, like your resume and LinkedIn profile.
  3. Apply to 10 to 15 job postings.
  4. Engage in 3 to 6 interview rounds.
  5. Secure a job offer.

Alternatively, if you have a strong network, you might choose a more streamlined path:

  1. Update your resume and LinkedIn profile.
  2. Let your network know you're open to new opportunities.
  3. Accept invitations for informal discussions.
  4. Apply to 1 or 2 targeted roles.
  5. Complete 1 or 2 interview rounds.
  6. Choose the best offer.

Yet, in a challenging job market — like the one in 2024 — these plans may lose momentum over time. A comprehensive job search strategy can be invaluable, especially when navigating career changes or new geographies. When faced with a prolonged search, common hurdles like procrastination, discouragement, imposter syndrome, and mental health challenges can emerge, making it harder to stay motivated.

Investing in planning, however, can bring renewed clarity, reduce uncertainties, and help you take control of the journey. Personally, I spent about two days creating my initial plan and only 4–6 hours a month adjusting it. This process can also be an empowering opportunity — a chance to envision and work toward your desired future.

Defining your goal

After being laid off from my role as an Engagement Manager, I felt a strong sense of disappointment. I valued that position deeply — the work environment was fantastic, my teammates were inspiring, and I saw many ways to continue making meaningful contributions. But after a few days, I decided to leave the past behind and focus on shaping a new future. Envisioning this future was energizing; the roles I found in job postings offered an average salary increase of 15% over my previous one, and many involved global projects.

With this inspiration, my initial goal came together naturally:

  • I took my last position as a benchmark, aiming for a 15% salary increase.
  • I set my timeline to align with my son's upcoming visit from Brazil.

This led to my goal statement: "I want to secure a remote role as a Project or Product Manager, or as an Engagement, Delivery, or Implementation Manager, working on global projects with an annual income 15% above my last salary, in a permanent position by February 9th, 2024."

However, as my deadline passed without reaching my goal, I had to reassess and adjust my approach. This meant broadening some criteria and prioritizing new aspects for my next goal. I gradually eased my initial constraints with each review, adapting more with each iteration.

Aspects to consider

  1. Salary Range: While a severance package allows you to aim high in your job search, once it ends, transitioning to employment insurance, a lower-paying survival job, or no income at all can strain your finances, deplete savings, and potentially lead to debt. I'll cover this topic in more detail in a future article. Still, it's essential to calculate your financial limits, set clear milestones, and adjust your salary expectations to avoid substantial impacts on your finances.
  2. Full-time or Part-time: While a full-time role provides income for all available work hours, part-time work, even at a higher hourly rate, often leads to a reduced overall income. When considering part-time positions, evaluate them in line with your financial milestones set in the salary range. Additionally, a part-time role can provide motivational benefits, reduce the career gap, and may eventually lead to a full-time opportunity.
  3. Remote, hybrid, or in the office: While choosing between remote, hybrid, or in-office work often depends on personal preference and employer arrangements, remote work opens up a vast array of opportunities in other cities, provinces, and even countries. However, as options expand, so does the level of competition.
  4. Permanent or contract: Permanent positions typically offer a long-term commitment, a stable work environment, and opportunities for growth within the company. While contract roles may lack these aspects, they can provide the chance to step into new positions quickly, often filling in for someone on leave or meeting immediate project needs.
  5. Employee or Incorporated: Starting a corporation can seem daunting for professionals like me who have primarily worked as employees. However, establishing a corporation is often straightforward and relatively quick, even though it comes with a learning curve and additional management responsibilities. Incorporating can also open up opportunities for temporary or contract roles that may not be available to traditional employees.

The goal isn't to cover every possible aspect to consider but rather to highlight key factors that can support you in defining your goals. These considerations will also help you refine your alerts on job portals.

Zone of Genius

Employers often have a lower risk tolerance during job market downturns, seeking candidates who closely match their needs. The Zone of Genius exercise can help you identify where you excel in ways that lead to both personal fulfillment and high performance. This is the area where your work is both energizing and leverages your unique talents to their fullest. In short, aim to find the zone where you are an exceptional talent and can be a top competitor. Then, look for job descriptions that strongly align with this zone.

To illustrate, here is a summary of my zone of genius:

  1. Project Management & Execution in Complex Environments
  2. Customer-Centric Problem Solving
  3. Team Leadership & Mentorship
  4. Process Improvement & Operational Efficiency
  5. Innovative & Strategic Thinking in Business Development

SMART goal

Finally, compile your definitions into a SMART goal statement:

  1. Specific: Be as specific as possible about your target role, industry, and location.
  2. Measurable: While the goal itself is binary (either achieved or not), tracking progress metrics can be helpful. I'll cover valuable metrics to monitor your progress in another article.
  3. Achievable: Set realistic expectations and adjust them as you gather more information during your search.
  4. Relevant: The relevance is clear — finding a new job is crucial when you're currently unemployed.
  5. Time-Bound: Set a realistic timeline that respects your mental health. Close deadlines can create pressure and stress, while longer timelines can help you maintain momentum without burnout.

Use the goal statement as your mantra, your battle cry.

In closing

A strategic job search plan empowers you to navigate even the toughest job markets with clarity and purpose. By defining clear goals, understanding key aspects of the roles you seek, and identifying your unique Zone of Genius, you set yourself up for a fulfilling career that aligns with your strengths and aspirations. Remember, a well-defined SMART goal becomes your roadmap, guiding each step forward and fueling your motivation along the way. As you move ahead, use these insights as your foundation — and stay tuned for the following article, where we'll delve into the power of self-discovery to elevate your journey further.
