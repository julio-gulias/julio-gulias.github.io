Reclaim Your Thinking Autonomy with Critical Thinking.

Tools, frameworks, dashboards, and AI can support better decisions — but they should not replace the responsibility to think, question, evaluate, and lead with judgment.

Critical Thinking Leadership AI Judgment

Originally published on LinkedIn on August 2, 2024.

I attended the Critical Thinking for Better Judgment and Decision-Making training some years ago, which significantly clarified many aspects of critical thinking. I strongly recommend attending this short but impactful training.

In today's world, where everything seems to be labelled as black or white, reclaiming our thinking autonomy has never been more crucial. As social media and para-social relationships influence our perceptions, losing sight of independent thought is easy. The Critical Thinking for Better Judgment and Decision Making course helps you break free from automatic alignments and fosters a mindset of thoughtful analysis.

Why attend?

This course offers powerful tools to restore your autonomy and enhance your decision-making skills:

  • Distinguish Fact from Fiction: Learn how to identify reliable information and separate it from falsehoods.
  • Contextual Knowledge Transfer: Gain the ability to apply what you know effectively across various contexts.
  • Ask the Right Questions: Develop the skill to inquire meaningfully and seek a deeper understanding.
  • Challenge Assumptions: Break away from preconceived notions and foster a culture of curiosity.

Beware of the top 5 critical thinking killers

  • Over-reliance on Authority
  • Black and White Thinking
  • Hasty Moral Judgments
  • Labels
  • Resistance to Change

Course highlights

  • Diverse Thinking Approaches: Explore critical, creative, and strategic thinking to expand your perspectives.
  • Master the Most Valuable Questions (MVQ): Learn to identify purpose, ask insightful questions, and analyze information from the wisest viewpoints.
  • Reflective Skepticism: Cultivate the ability to question facts, detect biases, and uncover hidden assumptions.
  • System 1 and System 2 Thinking: Discover when to rely on fast, automatic thinking and when to engage in slow, effortful deliberation.

Equip yourself with the tools to navigate a world filled with complexities and uncertainties. This course is an excellent pathway to becoming a more autonomous thinker and an effective decision-maker.

Thanks, Becki Saltzman, for this impactful training.

To those who decide to attend this training, enjoy it!
